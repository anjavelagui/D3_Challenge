    Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

    Report

    • As it related to poverty, we can see there is a slightly positive correlation between states' obesity rates and the percentage of the states' population living in poverty.

    • Age and Obesity do not appear to have a meaningful correlation at the state level. In better terms, obesity rates are lower in states with a higher Median Household Income.

    • When it comes to Smoking and Age, it appears that most states fall within a ten-year age range for their median smoker's age. Only 4 of the datapoints in the entire dataset fall outside of ages 32 to 42. Similar to Obesity and Smoking, we see a strong positive correlation between the percentage of residents lacking healthcare and the percentage of residents in poverty.
